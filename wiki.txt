"India, officially the Republic of India (Bhārat Gaṇarājya),[See also: Official names of India\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Names_of_the_Republic_of_India_in_its_official_languages]] is a country in South Asia\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia]. It is the seventh-largest\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependencies_by_area] country by area, the second-most populous\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population] country (with over 1.2 billion people\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_India]), and the most populous democracy [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/democracy] in\nthe world. It is bounded by the Indian Ocean [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean] on the south, the Arabian Sea\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Sea] on the southwest, and the Bay of Bengal [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal] on\nthe southeast. It shares land borders with Pakistan [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan] to the west;[1 = The Government of\nIndia [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_India] also regards Afghanistan [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan] as a\nbordering country, as it considers all of Kashmir [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashmir] to be part of India. However, this is \ndisputed [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashmir_conflict], and the region bordering Afghanistan is administered by Pakistan.\nSource: Ministry of Home Affairs (Department of Border Management) ] China [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China], Nepal\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal], and Bhutan [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutan] to the northeast; and Myanmar (Burma)\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar] and Bangladesh [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh] to the east. In the Indian Ocean,\nIndia is in the vicinity of Sri Lanka [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka] and the Maldives\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives]. India's Andaman and Nicobar Islands\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andaman_and_Nicobar_Islands] share a maritime border with Thailand\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand] and Indonesia [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia]. Its capital is New Delhi\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Delhi]; other metropolises [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_India_by_population] \ninclude Mumbai [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mumbai], Kolkata [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolkata], Chennai\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chennai], Bangalore [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangalore], Hyderabad\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad] and Ahmedabad [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmedabad].\n\nHome to the ancient Indus Valley Civilisation [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation] and a region of historic\ntrade routes and vast empires [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_empires_in_India], the Indian subcontinent\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_subcontinent] was identified with its commercial and cultural wealth for much of its long\nhistory.[Stein1998pp = 16–17] Four religions [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_religions]–Hinduism\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism_in_India], Buddhism [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Buddhism_in_India], Jainism\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jainism_in_India], and Sikhism [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikhism_in_India]–originated in India,\nwhereas Zoroastrianism [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism_in_India], Judaism [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaism], \nChristianity [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_India], and Islam [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_India] arrived\nin the first millennium CE, and they also played a part in shaping the region's diverse culture\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_India]. Gradually annexed and brought under the administration of the British East India\nCompany [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Company_rule_in_India] from the early 18th century and administered directly by the United\nKingdom [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj] after the Indian Rebellion of 1857\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857], it became an independent nation in 1947 after a struggle for independence\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_independence_movement] that was marked by non-violent resistance\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/non-violent_resistance] led by Mahatma Gandhi [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi].\n\nIn 2015, the Indian economy [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_India] was the world's seventh largest\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(nominal)] by nominal GDP\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product] and third largest\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_(PPP)] by purchasing power parity\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/purchasing_power_parity].(ref: imf2)market-based economic reforms\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_liberalisation_in_India] in 1991, India became one of the fastest-growing major economies\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_real_GDP_growth_rate_(latest_year)] and is considered a newly industrialised\ncountry [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/newly_industrialised_country]. However, it continues to face the challenges of poverty\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/poverty_in_India], corruption [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_India], malnutrition\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malnutrition_in_India], and inadequate public healthcare\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Healthcare_in_India]. A nuclear weapons state [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/nuclear_weapons_state] \nand regional power [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_(international_relations)#Power_as_status], it has the third largest\nstanding army [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_number_of_troops] in the world and ranks sixth in military\nexpenditure [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_military_expenditures] among nations. India is a federal\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federalism] constitutional republic [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/constitutional_republic] governed\nunder a parliamentary system [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/parliamentary_system] and consists of 29 states and 7 union territories\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/States_and_territories_of_India]. It is a pluralistic\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pluralism_(political_philosophy)], multilingual and multi-ethnic society and is also home to a\ndiversity of wildlife [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_India] in a variety of protected habitats.\n[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protected_areas_of_India]"
